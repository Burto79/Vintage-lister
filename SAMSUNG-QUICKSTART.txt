          📱 SAMSUNG PHONE → MAC - SUPER QUICK SETUP
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

🎯 FASTEST WAY (2 minutes):

  1. On Samsung: Take photos of vintage item

  2. On Samsung: Open Gmail/Email app
     → Attach photos
     → Send to yourself

  3. On Mac: Open Gmail
     → Download photos to Downloads folder

  4. On Mac Terminal:
     cd vintage-lister
     ./start.sh
     Choose option 1
     Drag photos in and press Enter

  ✅ DONE! See your AI-generated listing!

⚡ AUTOMATIC WAY (One-time 5 min setup, then auto forever):

  Step 1: INSTALL GOOGLE DRIVE ON MAC

    → Go to: https://www.google.com/drive/download/
    → Download "Drive for desktop"
    → Install and sign in with your Google account
       (Same account as your Samsung phone)

  Step 2: ENABLE BACKUP ON SAMSUNG

    → Open Google Photos app on Samsung
    → Tap your profile picture (top right)
    → Tap "Turn on backup"
    → Choose "High quality" (free unlimited!)

  Step 3: FIND YOUR PHOTOS FOLDER ON MAC

    Open Terminal and run:

    ls ~/Library/CloudStorage/

    Look for: GoogleDrive-youremail@gmail.com

    Your photos will be at:
    ~/Library/CloudStorage/GoogleDrive-youremail@gmail.com/My Drive/Google Photos

  Step 4: UPDATE .env FILE

    Edit vintage-lister/.env and change WATCH_FOLDER to:

    WATCH_FOLDER=/Users/alburtalvarado/Library/CloudStorage/GoogleDrive-YOUREMAIL/My Drive/Google Photos

    (Replace YOUREMAIL with your actual email folder name)

  Step 5: START WATCHING

    cd vintage-lister
    npm run dev watch

  NOW: Take photo on Samsung → It appears on Mac → Auto-analyzed! 🎉

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

🔌 USB CABLE WAY (If you have a cable handy):

  1. Install Android File Transfer:
     https://www.android.com/filetransfer/

  2. Connect Samsung to Mac with USB cable

  3. On Samsung: Swipe down notification
     → Tap "USB options"
     → Select "File Transfer"

  4. Android File Transfer opens
     → Go to DCIM/Camera
     → Drag photos to Mac Desktop

  5. Analyze them:
     cd vintage-lister
     npm run analyze -- ~/Desktop/*.jpg
